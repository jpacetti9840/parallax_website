Must Visit National Parks

Yellowstone National Park

Yellowstone National Park is located mainly in Wyoming, a little in Montana, and just a small section in Idaho. It is the world's first national park! Yellowstone is 2.2 million acres with various unique hydrothermal and geological features. Yellowstone has a very diverse ecosytem in which you can see bison, elk, mountain goats, otters, bears, moose, bald eagles, and many more animals. It also has beautiful scenery like the Grand Prismatic Spring and the Grand Canyon of the Yellowston River. Yellowstone also has half of the world's active geysers! Overall Yellowstone is a very unique must vist national park.

Grand Teton National Park

Grand Teton National Park was established in 1929. It is around 310,000 acres of beautiful landscape. The Grand Teton's have glacial lakes, winding rivers, diverse wildlife, and the mountains that stretch for 40 miles. The Grand Teton's are located in Jackson Hole valley. It is a has amazing scenery and over 250 miles of hiking trails to choose from. The Grand Teton National Park is very distinctive and unsurpassed in it's beauty, it's a great place to visit because it is so close to the Yellowstone National Park.

Rocky Mountain National Park

Rocky Mountain National Park was founded in 1915. It is located in Colorado and is 415 square miles or 265,807 acres. Rocky Mountain is one of the country's top wildlife watchng destinations becauses it houses over 60 species of mamals like elk, bighorn sheep, and moose. It also has an extensive mueseum collection that has 33,465 cultural objects, 294 works of art, 10,495 biological specimens and 455 geological specimens, a great place to go if you want to learn the history of the park. The Rocky Mountains are beautiful and full of fun things to do like hiking, biking, fishing, horseback riding, camping, and mountaineering.
